    The Meng Family

    <The Meng Family> is the first solo exhibition about pandas by artist Grey (@Panda by Grey, Greyworks) currently based out of Seoul, Republic of Korea, featuring the mother panda Meng Meng and her cubs as the main characters.

    Although, in general, pandas are separated from their mother and siblings from an early age, luckily Meng Meng and her children all gathered at the Beijing Zoo last fall. This was a very special event for panda fans, including me. I thought it would be a meaningful and happy moment for the Meng family, so decided to leave it as artworks of art to commemorate it. This is why I planned and prepared this exhibition.

    I designed this special exhibition to aesthetically and symbolically record and memorize the happy moments of the panda family as beautiful artworks. To this end, the artworks were created by combining the existing panda Meng Meng's family and masterpieces that are much loved by people all over the world. It was used the masterpieces of impressionist artists Vincent van Gogh, Pierre-Auguste Renoir, Paul Cézanne, Oscar-Claude Monet, and Georges-Pierre Seurat who expressed beautiful and intensely colors as the background elements of this work. The images of the Meng family being in harmony each background tell us their warm and touched story.

    These artworks feature totally six pandas; mother Meng Meng, first Meng Da and his twin brother Meng Er, third Meng Lan, fourth Meng Bao and her twin sister Meng Yu. I reflected their identity by expressing each panda's lovely characters realistically. Because they are so precious just the way they are, I certainly think these artistic records are very meaningful not only panda fans but also our world.

    Through my artworks, I hope that the loveliness of the pandas, the preciousness of the family, and the happy and warm feelings will be conveyed to many people.

Artwork 1

Title: Mother Panda and a Sunflower (萌萌, méng meng)

This artwork is based on mother Meng Meng as the main character and Vincent van Gogh's masterpiece <Wheat Field with Cypresses (1889)> as a background motif. Lovely Meng Meng looks at us with a sunflower, which is a representative flower in many of Gogh's masterpieces. Her tender smile and beautiful natural scenery make our minds comfortable. Through this artwork, I hope that we can get away from the present for a while and feel peaceful emotions.

Artwork 2

Title: Panda Brothers on the Skiff (萌大, méng dà  &  萌二, méng èr)

This artwork is based on Meng Da, the first son of Meng Meng, and his twin brother Meng Er, and Pierre-Auguste Renoir's masterpiece <The Seine at Asnieres (The Skiff) (1875)> as a background motif. The panda brothers are riding a boat together and the beautiful scenery around them will make the viewers smile around their mouths. Why don't you feel the breeze blowing from the riverside through this scene?

Artwork 3

Title: Smiling Panda and the Basket of Apples (萌兰, méng lán)

This artwork is based on Meng Meng's third son, Meng Lan, and Paul Cézanne's masterpiece <The Basket of Apples (1890-1894)> as a background motif. He laughs well and has the nickname smiling boy, and is a panda that delivers the happiness to many people. In particular, he likes apples, and he shows such characteristics well in this work. Make your day happy today with a smile from Meng Lan!

Artwork 4

Title: Lovely Panda Sisters (萌宝, méng bǎo  &  萌玉, méng yù)

This artwork is based on Meng Meng's twin daughters Meng Bao and Meng Yu as main characters, and Oscar-Claude Monet's masterpiece <Au Jardin, la famille de l'artiste (1875)> as a background motif. In order to express two cute and lovely sisters who are loved by many panda fans in a piece of artwork, I drew them surrounded by beautiful flowers. They seem to be talking to us with glistening eyes.

Artwork 5

Title: A Sunday Afternoon of The Meng Family

This artwork is based on mother Meng Meng and her five children as main characters, and Georges-Pierre Seurat's masterpiece <A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte (1884-1886)> as a background motif. The five children were expressed in the form of cute and adorable babies. I wanted to represent the happy and beautiful moment of The Meng Family through warm light and natural colors.
